Causes of Atherosclerosis

Cholesterol, Obesity, Smoking, Tobacco, Diabetes, High Triglycerides

There are many possibilities of how someone may have developed atherosclerosis. High blood pressure is a common cause for this disease because the surface area of the vessel is decreased. This disease takes a long time to develop; it can start is childhood and not affect you until late adulthood. There are nearly and sometimes more than 3 million cases of this a year in just the United States. Predictably, high cholesterol is another cause because cholesterol is something that blocks up the artery walls. Other causes include high triglycerides, a type of fat/lipid in the blood, and also obesity, tobacco, diabetes, smoking, and also inflammations from other diseases. These diseases can be coronary heart disease, lupus, and arthritis. It can also include random or unknown infections.
